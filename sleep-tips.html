Expert Sleeping Tips

for a Restful Night

Tired of counting sheep? Wish you could wake up feeling refreshed instead of groggy? We get it! A good night's sleep can sometimes feel impossible. That's why we've created the Sleep Lab – your guide to better sleep with practical tips backed by experts.

Unlocking Better Sleep

Top Tips From Our Experts

A good night's sleep doesn't happen by accident. One of the most important ways to get a good night's sleep is a consistent sleep schedule.

How can you do this? Here are a few easy sleeping tips to train your body for better nights rest:

  • Ditch the devices. Studies show that blue light from our devices disrupts the brain's production of melatonin, the sleep hormone. Turning everything off at least an hour before bed to help your body associate darkness with sleep.

  • Be mindful of what you consume. Caffeine stays in your system between four to six hours, so avoid it in the afternoon and evening. Alcohol might make you feel sleepy at first, but it acts as a diuretic, increasing the need to urinate and causing you to wake up more frequently throughout the night.

Creating the Perfect

Sleep Environment

Your sleep environment impacts all your senses. When sight, sound, and comfort levels are just right, your body and mind are more likely to naturally relax into a deep sleep.

Here's how to optimise it for the best rest possible:

  1. The right mattress. A comfortable, supportive mattress is super important! A quality mattress aligns your spine, reduces pressure points, and helps you stay asleep without aches and pains. Explore our range of Therapedic, Hypnos, and Tommy Bahama mattresses to find one that's perfect for you.

  2. Room temperature. Most people sleep best in a cool room (around 18 degrees Celsius). This aligns with the body's natural temperature drop during sleep.

  3. Embrace darkness. Your brain associates darkness with sleep. Blackout curtains or an eye mask can block light that disrupt melatonin production.

  4. Quiet zone. Minimising noise is important for a good night's sleep. Use earplugs or a white noise machine to create a peaceful environment for falling and staying asleep.

Mind and Body

Preparing for Restful Slumber

The hours before bedtime set the stage for your quality of sleep. Here's how to unwind and prepare:

  • Relaxation techniques. Practice deep breathing, mindfulness meditation, or gentle yoga. These techniques activate the body's relaxation response, lowering stress hormones and preparing you for sleep.

  • Manage stress. Stress is the enemy of sleep. Find healthy ways to cope with daily stressors, like exercise, journaling, or spending time in nature.

  • Wind-down ritual. Create a calming pre-bed routine. A warm bath, soothing music, or light reading can signal your brain that it's time to relax.

The Role of Diet and

Exercise in Sleep Quality

What you eat and how active you are directly impact your sleep. A balanced diet rich in whole foods, fruits, and vegetables provides the nutrients your body needs for optimal sleep-wake cycles.  Be mindful of sugar and overly processed foods, which can lead to energy spikes and crashes that disrupt sleep. Regular exercise is also crucial for better sleep. It helps you fall asleep more easily, improves sleep quality, and increases the amount of deep sleep you get.

Navigating Sleep Challenges

When To Seek Professional Help

If the sleeping tips above don't help, or if you experience any of the following, it's wise to consult a doctor or a somnologist (sleep specialist):

  • Persistent insomnia
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Snoring and sleep apnea
  • Unexplained sleep disruptions

A somnologist can diagnose any underlying conditions and recommend personalised treatment options to help you finally achieve a restful night's sleep!

Faqs

About Mattress Resources Australia’s Beds and Mattresses

What Are the Top Sleeping Tips To Improve Sleep Quality?

Focus on establishing a consistent sleep schedule – going to bed and waking up at the same time every day. 

Create a relaxing bedtime routine, manage stress, and prioritise a dark, cool, and quiet sleep environment. Limit caffeine, alcohol, and screen time before bed.

How Does the Mattress I Choose Affect My Sleep?

The right mattress provides essential support for proper spinal alignment, reducing pressure points that cause tossing and turning. 

A comfortable mattress that suits your preferred sleep position helps you fall asleep faster and stay asleep throughout the night.

Can Lifestyle Changes Really Make a Difference in How Well I Sleep?

Absolutely! Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and stress management techniques can all significantly improve sleep quality. 

Minimising daytime naps can also help regulate your nighttime sleep patterns.

What Should I Do if I’ve Tried All the Recommended Sleeping Tips and Still Can’t Sleep?

Consult your doctor or a sleep specialist. They can help identify any underlying medical conditions or sleep disorders that may be affecting your sleep.
